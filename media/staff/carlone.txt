Luca Carlone is a postdoctoral associate in the Laboratory for Information and Decision Systems at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. Before joining MIT, he was a postdoctoral fellow at Georgia Tech from 2013 to 2015. He got his Ph.D. from Politecnico di Torino, Italy, in 2012. His research interests include nonlinear estimation and optimization applied to mobile robot perception and planning. His contributions include theoretical analysis and practical applications of localization and mapping algorithms, as well as distributed optimization approaches in multi agent systems.
